What do we publish?

Editorials

Articles referring to research published elsewhere in that issue of the journal, or to important events in patient safety and the management of risk. Editorials are generally invited.

Personal Views

Articles pertinent to risk management, patient safety and medicolegal issues. Personal Views are generally invited.

Original Articles

Articles regarding the prevention and management of clinical risk. These should be between 2000–3000 words in total. Illustrations and tables must be included in the total words count (approx. 200 words each). Structured headings are required. All original articles submitted to the AJRM must contain an abstract, up to a maximum of 250 words.

Aims and Focus

A key focus of the Journal is supporting ARiMAs’ purpose which is to “help build strong and resilient companies, economies and societies in Africa.” Accordingly, when submitting your work to the Journal, it would be useful to the broader readership to highlight where possible any issues that is in line with the focus.

Editorial and Publishing Policies

Abstracts are required and must be submitted within the body of the article. Brief author bios are also required.

Although the Journal accepts complete articles for review, authors also are encouraged to submit a brief abstract and outline describing the subject and purpose of the article. (If you have written an article, you may submit the manuscript in addition to an abstract). Articles and abstracts should be submitted electronically via the Authors’ account.

Initial evaluation and feedback by the editorial review board.

All manuscripts undergo a thorough review process and the Editorial Board does not guarantee that a submission will be published. During this process, all authors are obliged to refrain from distributing their submission to others.

The submission will be reviewed by members of the Editorial Board. Following review, the author(s) will be advised of the Board’s decision. If an abstract is approved, the author(s) will be asked to submit a manuscript for review.

Subsequent evaluation and feedback.

Following initial evaluation, the author(s) may be asked to revise the manuscript. The Editorial Board will review the submission and may offer suggestions to help authors focus on the needs and interests of readers. All manuscripts accepted for publication in the Journal are subject to review and editing by the Editorial Board.

Manuscript guidelines.

Please note that manuscripts not conforming to the guidelines below may be returned.

Only manuscripts of sufficient quality that meet the aims and scope of the African Journal of Risk Management will be reviewed.

There are no fees payable to submit or publish in this journal.

As part of the submission process you will be required to warrant that you are submitting your original work, that you have the rights in the work, and that you have obtained and can supply all necessary permissions for the reproduction of any copyright works not owned by you, that you are submitting the work for first publication in the Journal and that it is not being considered for publication elsewhere and has not already been published elsewhere.

Manuscripts should be double-spaced, excluding references charts and figures, and must:

  • Include an abstract and brief author bios
  • Address issues specifically for risk managers in Africa (See “Aims and Focus” above)
  • Present new information or add a new slant to existing literature
  • Be research-based or process-based
  • Be technically accurate
  • Ideally be 3,500 to 7,000 words in length

Cover Letter

Submissions should be accompanied by a cover letter including a brief biographical sketch of the author(s) including names (in the order they are to appear), titles, affiliations and locations, along with complete contact information for each author (address, phone and email address).

References should conform to the following style:

  • Journal Articles:

    • Brennan TA, Leape LL, Laird N, Hebert L, Localio AR, Lawthers AG. Incidence of adverse events and negligence in hospitalized patients: Results of the Harvard Medical Practice Study I. N Engl J Med. 1991;324:377-384.
  • Books:
    • 2. Aspden P, Corrigan JM, Wolcott J, Erickson SM. Patient safety: Achieving a new standard of care. Washington, DC: National Academies Press; 2004.
  • Journal Articles on the Internet:
    • 3. Wilson JS, Miller JB, O'Neil MW. Crossing the quality chasm: A new health system for the 21st century. Am Psychol. 1992;48(6):650-672. http://www.aopa.org/journals/wilson.html. Accessed April 12, 2002.
